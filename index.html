Estátua do Laçador

A symbol of Gaúcho tradition

O Laçador is a representation of the Gaúcho. It was defined as the official symbol of Porto Alegre in 1992 via municipal law. The statue was created by sculptor Antônio Caringi. The statue was deemed historic heritage in 2001. In 2007, the monument was transfered from its original place, Largo do Bombeiro, to the Sítio O Laçador, in front of the Salgado Filho Airport.

Information about the statue

  • Created in 1992, by sculptor Antônio Caringi
  • The statue is 6.55m tall
  • Made of bronze, its pedestal is made of granite
  • The statue is wearing traditional Gaúcho clothes and a loop
  • Based on traditionalist Paixão Côrtes
